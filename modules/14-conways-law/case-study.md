# Module 14 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Apply Conway's Law to a real org-to-architecture coupling you've actually
> lived. The goal isn't to indict anyone's org chart — it's to practice reading architecture
> as a shadow of communication structure, and to see where you could intervene on the org to
> change the system.

## The system and the org

_Name one real system you know well and the organization that built it. Describe both in a
sentence: what the system does, and how the teams that own it are arranged._

## The two graphs

_Sketch the org communication graph and the architecture graph (link an image or describe
them). Draw org edges where teams actually communicate, not where the chart says they should._

## The homomorphism

_Walk the mapping: for each major interface in the architecture, name the team boundary that
produced it. Where is the mirror faithful?_

## Where the mirror leaks

_Find one place the two graphs disagree — a shared module no team owns, a service split
across two teams, duplicated logic because two teams don't talk. What friction does it cause?_

## The Inverse Conway Maneuver you'd propose

_Design a team-boundary change to produce the architecture you actually want. Name the team
types, the interaction mode, each team's API, and the change in cognitive load. Predict the
architecture that would grow along the new seams — and the delay before it does._

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did reading the architecture as an org shadow surface that you
hadn't seen when you looked at the code alone?_
