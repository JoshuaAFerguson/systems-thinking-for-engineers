# Module 01 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. Name the three things every system has. Which is usually easiest to see, and which is
   usually most important to its behavior?
2. What's the difference between a system's *function* and its *purpose* in Meadows'
   language — and how do you figure out either one?
3. Why is "watch what it does, not what it says" the rule for identifying purpose?
4. Rank these by how much each changes a system's behavior: swapping an element, rewiring an
   interconnection, changing the purpose. Why that order?
5. Give an example from your own work where the stated purpose and the revealed purpose of a
   system were different.
6. What does it mean to say a system boundary is an *invention* rather than a fact? Give a
   consequence of drawing one badly.
7. If you could change only one of {elements, interconnections, purpose} to fix a system you
   care about, which would you choose, and why?
