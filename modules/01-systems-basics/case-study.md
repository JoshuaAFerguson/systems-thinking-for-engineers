# Module 01 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Map the elements, interconnections, and purpose of a real system you know.
> The goal is to practice separating the parts you can see from the relationships that
> actually drive behavior — and to catch the purpose hiding underneath the stated one.

## The system

_Pick one real system you know well — a service, a team, a pipeline, a product. Name it and
describe it in a sentence._

## Elements

_List the parts you can see and name. Keep it to the ones that matter._

## Interconnections

_Now the relationships: what information, signals, code, money, or rules flow between the
elements? What depends on what?_

## Function or purpose

_What is this system actually for — deduced from its behavior over time, not its stated
goal? If the stated and revealed purposes differ, name both._

## The boundary I drew

_Where did you put the boundary, and what did that choice leave out? What sits just outside
the frame that still shapes the behavior?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did naming the three components surface that you hadn't seen
before?_
