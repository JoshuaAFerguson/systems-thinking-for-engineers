# Module 06 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Reconstruct a real decision where a wrong mental model cost the team. The
> goal is to see the model *behind* the decision — the selected data and untested assumptions
> that felt like plain facts at the time.

## The decision

_Name a real decision that went sideways because someone (maybe you) was running a flawed
mental model. Describe it in a sentence._

## The model in play

_What picture of how-things-work was the decider holding? State the assumptions it rested on._

## Walking the ladder

_Reconstruct the climb: what observable data existed, what got selected, what meaning and
assumptions were layered on, and what conclusion and action resulted?_

## The reflexive loop

_How did the existing belief bias which data got noticed in the first place? Where did the
model confirm itself?_

## What inquiry would have changed

_Where could advocacy have been swapped for inquiry, or an assumption tested against data?
What would that have surfaced in time?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did examining the model — rather than the decision — reveal
about how the team reasons?_
