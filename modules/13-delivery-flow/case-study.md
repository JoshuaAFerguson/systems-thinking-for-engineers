# Module 13 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Analyze a real delivery pipeline you've worked in as a system of **flows and
> queues**. Put real numbers on it where you can — Little's Law rewards measurement. The goal is
> to find the one constraint that sets the pace and see the invisible queues around it. Use the
> stocks-and-flows tools from Module 02 and the leverage points from Module 08.

## The pipeline

_Name one real delivery process you know — from idea to production. Describe its main stages in a
sentence._

## The queues (invisible stocks)

_List the queues where work waits: PR review, QA, deploy, approvals. For each, is it visible to
the people who own the work, or hidden?_

## Little's Law, measured

_Pick one queue. Record L (WIP), λ (throughput), and W (lead time) — measure two, derive the
third. Show the arithmetic. Does the number match how long the wait *feels*?_

## The constraint

_Which single step sets the pace? What piles up in front of it? Walk through identify → exploit →
subordinate → elevate for this system — what would you actually do at each step?_

## WIP and batch size

_Where is WIP too high or batches too large? Predict, via Little's Law, what a WIP cap or smaller
batch would do to lead time. What feedback would speed up?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did measuring the flow surface that the team's felt sense of
"we're busy" was hiding?_
