# Module 09 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. Why do you write the model's **purpose** before choosing its stocks and flows?
2. Write the discrete-time update rule for a stock in one line. What are its inputs?
3. At what exact point does a stock-and-flow model stop producing straight-line behavior — what
   turns it into a loop?
4. What are the steps of the **modeling loop**, and why is it a loop rather than a pipeline?
5. Where in the loop does the actual *learning* happen — the first run, or somewhere else?
6. What does "models are for insight, not prediction" mean? Give a way you could misuse a model
   by treating it as a forecast.
7. In your SaaS/backlog/debt model, what equilibrium (if any) did it approach, and which loop
   drove it there?
8. What was the one surprise your simulation produced, and what did it correct in your intuition?
