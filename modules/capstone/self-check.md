# Capstone — Self-Check

Answer these honestly after finishing your [analysis](analysis.md). They aren't a quiz on
theory — they're a mirror on the analysis you just produced. If an answer is uncomfortable,
that's the part worth reworking.

1. **Does the structure explain the behavior?** Can a reader look at your CLD / stock-and-flow
   model and see *why* the behavior-over-time graph looks the way it does? If not, what's the
   model missing?
2. **Are the leverage points real?** How many of your recommendations act on structure,
   feedback, rules, or goals rather than just tuning parameters? Are they ranked, and can you
   defend the ranking?
3. **Did you find a delay or non-linearity that a linear reading would miss?** Where?
4. **Is the boundary honest?** Where did you draw it, and how would the analysis change if you
   drew it somewhere else?
5. **What did the systems view reveal that you couldn't see before?** Name the specific
   insight that only showed up once you modeled the structure.
6. **What are you least sure about?** Which part of the analysis is speculative, and what would
   you need to observe to firm it up?
7. **What would you do differently** if you ran this analysis again — a different boundary, a
   different key variable, a different archetype lens?
8. **Looking back at your Module 00 purpose statement:** can you now do the thing you said you
   wanted to be able to do? What's still ahead?
