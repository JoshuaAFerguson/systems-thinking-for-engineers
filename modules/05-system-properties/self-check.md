# Module 05 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. Define **resilience**. Why is it not the same as stability, efficiency, or constancy?
2. What is **self-organization**, and why does Meadows call it the most powerful form of
   resilience?
3. What does **hierarchy** do for a system, and how does it keep complexity manageable?
4. Explain the **efficiency-vs-resilience trade-off**. What gets cut, and what bet are you
   making when you cut it?
5. What is **sub-optimization**, and how does hierarchy make it possible?
6. Too much central control or too much subsystem autonomy — what does each failure mode do
   to a hierarchy?
7. Give an example from your own work where resilience was traded away for efficiency, and
   say what disturbance would expose it.
