# Module 05 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Run a resilience audit on a real system. The goal is to see where its
> ability to recover actually lives, where it was quietly traded away for efficiency, and
> where a part is winning at the expense of the whole.

## The system

_Name one real system you depend on — a service, a team, a pipeline, a supply chain. Describe
it in a sentence._

## Resilience inventory

_What disturbances can it absorb and recover from? What are the loops or mechanisms that give
it that bounce-back?_

## Where it would break

_Name the disturbances it could not recover from. What's missing — a buffer, a backup, a
self-organizing capacity?_

## The efficiency trade

_Where has slack, redundancy, or self-organization been optimized away? What was gained, and
what became fragile as a result?_

## Sub-optimization check

_Is any subsystem pursuing its own goal at the expense of the whole? Describe the local goal
and the system-level cost._

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did the resilience lens surface that a performance or
uptime dashboard would not?_
