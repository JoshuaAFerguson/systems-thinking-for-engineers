# Module 04 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Take a delayed feedback you've lived through and trace how the delay (or a
> nonlinearity) produced surprise. The goal is to see the oscillation or overshoot as a
> property of the *structure*, not of anyone's competence.

## The system

_Name a real system with a delayed feedback — autoscaling lag, a hiring pipeline, alert
fatigue, an inventory or restock loop. Describe it in a sentence._

## The loop and its delay

_Sketch or describe the feedback loop and mark where the delay lives — between action and
effect, or between the stock changing and anyone perceiving it._

## The behavior over time

_What did the system actually do? Oscillate? Overshoot and collapse? Sketch or describe the
BOTG._

## The nonlinearity (if any)

_Is there a threshold or cliff in play — a point where response stopped being proportional?
Describe it._

## Bounded rationality at work

_What limited or delayed information were the actors working from? Were their decisions
locally reasonable even though the outcome was bad?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did the delay/nonlinearity lens reveal that "someone messed up"
would have missed — and what structural change would help?_
