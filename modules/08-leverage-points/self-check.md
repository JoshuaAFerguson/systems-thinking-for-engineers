# Module 08 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. Roughly in order, what are Meadows' twelve leverage points? (Get the *shape* — parameters at
   the bottom, paradigms near the top — even if you miss a rung.)
2. What is the essay's central, counterintuitive claim about where people intervene?
3. Why are **numbers/parameters** (point 12) usually the *weakest* place to intervene, even
   though they're where everyone starts?
4. Give an example of a high-leverage **information flow** (point 6) intervention from your own
   world. Why is it so cheap yet so powerful?
5. Why does Meadows rank **goals** (3) and **paradigms** (2) above rules and loops?
6. What does "the power to transcend paradigms" (point 1) actually mean in practice?
7. Explain her warning that even correct leverage points are often pushed in the *wrong
   direction* — give one example.
8. For a problem you own, what's a higher-leverage intervention than the obvious one?
