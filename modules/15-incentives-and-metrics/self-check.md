# Module 15 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. Why is a metric a *feedback signal* and not just an observation? What changes the moment
   you start rewarding it?
2. State Goodhart's Law. State Campbell's Law. How do they differ from "the metric is just
   inaccurate"?
3. What is a proxy metric, and why does the proxy drift from the goal *specifically once it
   becomes a target*?
4. Explain the cobra effect in your own words, with an engineering example.
5. What does it mean for a local optimization to harm the global system? Give an example and
   name the fitting archetype (Module 07).
6. What is a balancing (paired) measure, and why does it make gaming visible?
7. State the one audit question you should ask before shipping any metric.
8. Where do metrics sit on Meadows' leverage ladder — and why are they both a powerful lever
   *and* a classic trap?
