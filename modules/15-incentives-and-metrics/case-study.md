# Module 15 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Apply this module's lens to a real metric in your org and its unintended
> consequences. The goal isn't to name-and-shame a dashboard — it's to practice tracing a
> measure from goal, to proxy, to behavior, to system effect, and to find the balancing
> measure that was missing.

## The metric

_Name one real metric that drove behavior in your world — a KPI, an SLA, an OKR, a
leaderboard, a bonus trigger. State what goal it was meant to serve._

## Goal vs. proxy

_What did the metric actually measure, and how far did that proxy sit from the real goal?
Where was the gap that gaming could slip through?_

## The behavior it rewarded

_What did people (or teams, or services) actually do in response? Describe the behavior the
metric rewarded — including the parts nobody intended._

## The unintended consequence

_Trace the cobra effect. What got worse while the number looked better? Draw or describe the
loop (Module 03) and name the archetype (Module 07) if one fits._

## Local win, global loss?

_Did optimizing this metric in one place push cost somewhere else in the system? Where?_

## The fix that was missing

_What balancing measure, or better proxy, would have made the gaming visible or unrewarding?
Where does your fix land on the leverage ladder (Module 08)?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did tracing the metric as a feedback signal surface that the
dashboard hid?_
