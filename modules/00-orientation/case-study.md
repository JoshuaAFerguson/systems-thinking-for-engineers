# Module 00 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Apply this module's lens to a real system you've actually seen. The goal
> isn't to be right — it's to practice looking at relationships instead of parts. Later
> modules will give you sharper tools; here, just describe.

## The system

_Pick one real system you know well — a service, a team, a process, a product. Name it and
describe it in a sentence._

## Reductionist view

_List its parts and what each one does, the way you'd draw it on a whiteboard._

## Systemic view

_Now redraw it as relationships. What flows between the parts? What depends on what? Where
does an output loop back to become an input?_

## Where linear thinking would fail here

_Describe one decision someone might make about this system using simple cause-and-effect
reasoning — and why it would backfire. What feedback, delay, or nonlinearity would bite?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did switching lenses surface that you hadn't seen before?_
