# Module 00 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. In one sentence, what makes a set of parts a *system* rather than just a pile of parts?
2. When is reductionism the *right* tool, and when does it mislead you?
3. Define, without notes: **element**, **interconnection**, **purpose**, **feedback**,
   **emergence**.
4. Give an example of emergence from your own work.
5. Name the three features of systems that break simple cause-and-effect reasoning.
6. Why does drawing the system **boundary** in a different place change the answer you get?
7. What did you write as your purpose statement — and why that?
