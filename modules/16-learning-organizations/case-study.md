# Module 16 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Apply this module's lens to a real learning (or non-learning) loop in a team
> you know. The goal isn't to grade the team — it's to practice seeing whether the loop
> actually closes, and whether the fixes touch actions or the rules that generate actions.

## The team and the loop

_Name a real team and a specific loop it runs — postmortems, retros, code review, a quarterly
planning ritual. Describe what the loop is supposed to learn._

## The five disciplines, briefly

_For this team, which of Senge's five disciplines are present and which are missing? Point to
evidence, not vibes._

## A recurring failure (or a real learn)

_Describe either a failure this team keeps repeating, or a time it genuinely learned and
never repeated a mistake. What was different in the loop each time?_

## Single-loop or double-loop?

_Look at the fixes the team actually made. Did they change actions, or did they question the
governing variables? Classify a few concretely._

## Where the loop is severed (or holds)

_Walk the loop — signal → analysis → change → verification — and mark where it breaks (or why
it holds). Is blame corrupting the analysis? Do action items die unverified?_

## The double-loop fix that's missing

_What governing variable would the team have to question to actually stop the recurrence?
Where does that intervention land on the leverage ladder (Module 08)?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did looking at the team as a learning *loop* surface that a
list of action items hid?_
