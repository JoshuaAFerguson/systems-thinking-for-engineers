# Module 16 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. Name Senge's five disciplines. Why is systems thinking called the *fifth* — the one that
   integrates the rest?
2. Define single-loop and double-loop learning. Use the thermostat to explain the difference.
3. Given a real fix, how do you tell whether it was single-loop or double-loop?
4. Why does a team that only ever does single-loop learning keep repeating the same class of
   failure?
5. What are the four links of a working incident/learning loop, and what happens if any one
   is severed?
6. Why is a blameless postmortem a *loop-design* decision and not just a matter of tone?
   (Tie it to information flows, Module 15.)
7. Which system archetypes (Module 07) does chronic non-learning tend to look like from the
   outside?
8. Where does double-loop learning sit on Meadows' leverage ladder (Module 08), and why is it
   higher-leverage than a single-loop patch?
