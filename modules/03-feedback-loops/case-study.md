# Module 03 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Draw a causal loop diagram of a real dynamic you've watched play out. The
> goal is to make the feedback visible — to show, in arrows and signs, why the system did
> what it did instead of just narrating it.

## The dynamic

_Name a real behavior you've seen unfold over time — a growth spurt, a spiral, a system that
kept snapping back to the same level. Describe it in a sentence._

## The causal loop diagram

_List your variables, then the causal links between them with `+`/`−` polarity. Paste a
sketch or [LOOPY](https://ncase.me/loopy/) link. Label each loop **R** or **B**._

## The dominant loop

_Which loop drove the behavior you observed, and how do its link signs prove its polarity?_

## Where another loop lurks

_Name a second loop — balancing or reinforcing — that is present but weaker, and say what
would make it take over._

## Where linear thinking would fail here

_Describe a "do more of X" decision someone might make about this system, and how the
feedback loop would defeat it._

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did drawing the loop show you that the story alone didn't?_
