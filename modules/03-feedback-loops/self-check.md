# Module 03 — Self-Check

Answer from memory, then check yourself against the readings and your notes. If you can't
answer one cleanly, that's the part to revisit.

1. In one sentence each, what does a **balancing** loop do and what does a **reinforcing**
   loop do?
2. What kind of behavior over time does each loop produce (draw the BOTG shape in your head)?
3. On a CLD, what do a `+` and a `−` link each mean?
4. How do you determine whether a whole loop is reinforcing or balancing from its link signs?
5. Explain the thermostat loop and the viral-growth loop, and say which is which.
6. Why does a reinforcing loop almost never run forever in a real system? What stops it?
7. Name one balancing and one reinforcing loop from your own work.
