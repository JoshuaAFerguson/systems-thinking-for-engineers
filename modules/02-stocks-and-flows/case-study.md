# Module 02 — Case Study

> **Your work.** Pick a real stock in your world and model it as a bathtub. The goal is to
> get fluent at seeing accumulations and the rates that drive them — and to sketch the
> behavior over time before reaching for any math.

## The stock

_Name one real accumulation you care about — a backlog, tech debt, open incidents,
headcount, cash runway. Describe it and its unit in a sentence._

## The flows

_List every inflow that raises it and every outflow that lowers it. Give each a unit
(something-per-time)._

## The bathtub equation

_Write it as `stock = inflows − outflows`. Note which flows you actually control and which
you don't._

## Behavior over time

_Sketch or describe the stock's BOTG over a period you've observed. What shape is it, and
which flow imbalance produced that shape?_

## The delay

_Where does a delay live in this system? How long between turning a flow and seeing the stock
respond?_

## What I noticed

_Two or three sentences: what did modeling this as stock-and-flow reveal that a snapshot of
the number alone would hide?_
