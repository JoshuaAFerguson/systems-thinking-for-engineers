# Reading List & Tools

The sources this curriculum draws on. You don't need all of them — *Thinking in Systems*
is the spine; the rest are referenced by specific modules.

## Core texts

- **Donella H. Meadows — _Thinking in Systems: A Primer_** (2008). The spine of this
  curriculum. Stocks, flows, feedback, resilience, leverage points, system traps.
- **Peter Senge — _The Fifth Discipline_** (1990/2006). Mental models, systems thinking
  as a discipline, the learning organization, archetypes.
- **John D. Sterman — _Business Dynamics: Systems Thinking and Modeling for a Complex
  World_** (2000). The rigorous modeling reference; stock-and-flow, simulation.
- **Jay W. Forrester — _Industrial Dynamics_** (1961) & _Urban Dynamics_ (1969). The
  origin of system dynamics. Skim for lineage and intuition.

## Key essays & papers

- **Donella Meadows — "Leverage Points: Places to Intervene in a System"** (1999).
  The 12 leverage points. Free essay (Donella Meadows Project / Academy for Systems Change).
- **Donella Meadows — "Dancing with Systems"** (2001). How to act inside systems you
  can't fully control.
- **Daniel H. Kim — "Systems Archetypes I–III"** (Pegasus Communications). Compact
  archetype reference.
- **Chris Argyris — the Ladder of Inference** (via Senge's _Fieldbook_). Mental models.
- **Richard Cook — "How Complex Systems Fail"** (2000). Short, essential for reliability.
- **Nancy Leveson — _Engineering a Safer World_** (2011), STAMP model. Safety as a
  control problem. (Reliability module.)
- **Charles Goodhart / Marilyn Strathern — Goodhart's Law.** "When a measure becomes a
  target, it ceases to be a good measure." (Incentives & metrics module.)

## Engineering applications

- **Melvin Conway — "How Do Committees Invent?"** (1968). Conway's Law, original.
- **Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais — _Team Topologies_** (2019). Org-as-system for
  software delivery. (Conway's Law module.)
- **Eliyahu Goldratt — _The Goal_** (1984) and **Gene Kim et al. — _The Phoenix
  Project_** (2013). Theory of Constraints and flow, in novel form. (Delivery flow module.)
- **Donald Reinertsen — _The Principles of Product Development Flow_** (2009). Queues,
  WIP, batch size, Little's Law. (Delivery flow module.)
- **Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble, Gene Kim — _Accelerate_** (2018). Feedback and
  measurement in software delivery.

## Tools

- **[LOOPY](https://ncase.me/loopy/)** — browser tool for quick causal loop diagrams. Great
  for the mapping exercises. No install.
- **[Insight Maker](https://insightmaker.com/)** — free, browser-based stock-and-flow
  simulation. Good for the modeling module.
- **[Vensim PLE](https://vensim.com/free-download/)** — free-for-personal-use system
  dynamics modeling. More powerful; steeper.
- **Python** — for those who'd rather model in code: plain NumPy/Matplotlib, or
  [BPTK-Py](https://bptk.transentis.com/) / [PySD](https://pysd.readthedocs.io/) for
  system-dynamics-style models.
- **Any diagramming tool** (Excalidraw, draw.io, Mermaid) for causal loop diagrams you
  want to commit into a module as an image or `.mmd` file.
